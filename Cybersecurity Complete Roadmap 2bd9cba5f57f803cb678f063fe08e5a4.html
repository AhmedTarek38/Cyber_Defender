FAQ

‚ùì What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, misuse, or damage.

It covers everything from defending networks to testing vulnerabilities, managing risks, analyzing threats, and securing the cloud.

‚ùì Is Cybersecurity one job or many jobs?

Cybersecurity is not a single job ‚Äî it is a large ecosystem made up of many specialties and career paths.

Each specialty focuses on a different mission, skillset, and mindset.

üü¶ What is the Blue Team?

Blue Team refers to the defensive side of cybersecurity, responsible for monitoring, detecting, analyzing, and responding to security threats inside an organization.

According to SANS and CISA guidelines, Blue Teams work to maintain security posture, prevent breaches, and minimize impact during cyber incidents by using tools like SIEMs, EDR, firewalls, and threat intelligence.

Their mission is to protect systems, networks, and data from attacks and ensure continuous visibility across the environment.

üî¥ What is the Red Team?

Red Team is the offensive security group that simulates real-world cyberattacks to identify weaknesses before real attackers exploit them.

Based on principles from NIST and SANS Offensive Operations, Red Teams conduct ethical hacking, penetration testing, adversary simulation, and exploit development to test the resilience of systems and people.

Their mission is to think and operate like attackers in order to improve the organization‚Äôs defensive capabilities and uncover hidden vulnerabilities.

üü© What is GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance)?

GRC represents the management and strategic side of cybersecurity, focused on aligning security with business goals, evaluating risks, and ensuring compliance with global standards.

According to NIST RMF and ISO 27001 frameworks, GRC teams handle policy development, risk assessments, audits, regulatory compliance, data protection, and governance reporting.

Their mission is to ensure the organization operates securely, legally, and in accordance with international security frameworks, while managing risks effectively.
